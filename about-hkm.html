Hare Krishna Movement

A worldwide family united under the lotus feet of Srila Prabhupada

Our Eternal Spiritual Master

His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Srila Prabhupada is our Founder-Acharya and the sole Diksha Guru of this movement — guiding every devotee through his books, lectures, letters and recordings for all time to come.

Who We Are

The Hare Krishna Movement is a global Vaishnava community founded on the timeless teachings of Srila Prabhupada. Our mission is to spread pure devotional service — Bhakti Yoga — to every town and village, exactly as Srila Prabhupada instructed.

A Movement Built on Srila Prabhupada's Books

Srila Prabhupada translated and commented on more than 70 volumes of Vedic scripture — including the Bhagavad-Gita As It Is, the Srimad-Bhagavatam, and the Chaitanya-Charitamrita. These books serve as the unchanging foundation of everything we teach and practice.

Devotees across the world study these books daily, follow the four regulative principles, and chant a minimum of 16 rounds of the Hare Krishna maha-mantra every day — living proof that the path Srila Prabhupada gave us works in any culture or country.

"If you want to know Krishna, you must read my books. If you read my books, you will understand Krishna."
— Srila Prabhupada

Our History

From a single boat crossing the Atlantic to a worldwide spiritual family

1896

Birth of Srila Prabhupada

Abhay Charan De is born on 1 September in Calcutta (Kolkata), India, into a devoted Vaishnava family.

1922

Meeting His Guru

Abhay Charan meets Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Saraswati Thakura, who instructs him to spread Krishna consciousness in the English language.

1965

Journey to America

At 69 years old, Srila Prabhupada sails alone to New York on a cargo ship, carrying three crates of books and deep faith in his Guru's order.

1966

ISKCON Founded

The International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) is officially incorporated in New York City on 13 July 1966.

1970s

Global Expansion

Temples open across America, Europe, India, and beyond. Srila Prabhupada travels the globe 14 times, personally establishing communities and inspiring thousands.

1977

Eternal Departure

Srila Prabhupada departs from this world on 14 November in Vrindavan, India — leaving behind 108 temples, thousands of disciples, and an eternal library of Vedic knowledge.

1997

ISKCON Bangalore Established

ISKCON Bangalore at Hare Krishna Hill, Rajajinagar is established as a major centre of pure devotional service, strictly following Srila Prabhupada as the sole Diksha Guru.

2000s–Today

Hare Krishna Movement Worldwide

The Hare Krishna Movement grows to over 70 centres worldwide, with temples and communities across India, Europe, Asia, and beyond — all carrying Srila Prabhupada's message forward.

Srila Prabhupada — Our Diksha Guru

The Hare Krishna Movement follows the sacred principle that Srila Prabhupada continues to act as the initiating (Diksha) Guru for all devotees through his transcendental instructions preserved in his books, lectures, letters, and conversations.

Vani is Eternal

Srila Prabhupada's vani (words and instructions) are available to every sincere seeker and carry the full potency of transcendental initiation. He said: "Anyone who accepts my teachings is my disciple."

Pure Paramparā

Our initiation connects every devotee directly into the unbroken chain of disciplic succession (paramparā) coming from Lord Krishna himself, with Srila Prabhupada as the transparent via medium.

Universal Access

Regardless of nationality, language, or background, anyone anywhere in the world can receive Srila Prabhupada's mercy and take initiation through the Hare Krishna Movement centres.

Guiding Berlin

Here in Berlin, we carry the same torch. Our Saturday programs, Bhagavad-Gita classes, and kirtans are all conducted according to Srila Prabhupada's instructions and standards.

"Even after my physical disappearance I will be with you through my books and instructions. Follow them and you will be successful in your devotional service."
— Srila Prabhupada

Featured Temple

Gupt Vrindavan Dham

Sawai Madhopur, Rajasthan, India

Gupt Vrindavan Dham is one of Rajasthan's most magnificent Hare Krishna temples, dedicated to the divine service of Their Lordships under the guidance of Srila Prabhupada's teachings. The temple complex embodies the spirit of Vrindavan — the eternal abode of Lord Krishna — and serves as a major centre for pilgrimage, festivals, and devotional life.

Thousands of devotees visit this sacred dham each year to chant, learn from Srila Prabhupada's books, and experience the transcendental atmosphere of pure Bhakti Yoga.

Akshaya Patra — The Inexhaustible Pot

Inspired by Srila Prabhupada's vision that "no one within ten miles of a temple should go hungry," the Akshaya Patra Foundation has grown into the world's largest NGO school meal programme.

Feeding Millions Every Day

Akshaya Patra, initiated by ISKCON Bangalore, now serves over 2 million freshly cooked, nutritious mid-day meals to school children across India every single day. Since its founding in 2000, the programme has served over 4 billion meals.

The name comes from the divine pot in the Mahabharata that could produce unlimited food — a fitting symbol for Srila Prabhupada's teaching that Krishna's mercy is inexhaustible.
