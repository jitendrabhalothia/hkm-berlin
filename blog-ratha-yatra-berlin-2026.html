Ratha Yatra Berlin 2026 — Everything You Need to Know

The Festival of the Chariots comes to Berlin — free entry, free prasadam, kirtan all day.

What is Ratha Yatra?

Ratha Yatra — the Festival of the Chariots — is one of the oldest and most joyful festivals in the world. Originating in Puri, India, over 5,000 years ago, it celebrates Lord Jagannath (the Lord of the Universe) who comes out of his temple to bless all people equally, regardless of caste, religion or background.

The festival features a massive decorated chariot (ratha) that is pulled through the streets by devotees and guests. Anyone who pulls the rope of Lord Jagannath's chariot is said to receive immense spiritual blessings.

Berlin Ratha Yatra 2026 — Key Details

Date: Saturday, 18 July 2026

Time: 10:00 AM onwards

Location: Sri Ganesha Hindu Temple, Hasenheide 106, 10967 Berlin

Entry: Completely FREE — no ticket needed

Prasadam: Free vegan & vegetarian food for 600+ guests

What Will Happen on the Day?

  • Kirtan (devotional chanting) — live music with mridanga drums, kartals and harmonium throughout the day
  • Chariot procession — Lord Jagannath, Baladeva and Subhadra on a beautifully decorated chariot
  • Bhagavad-Gita talks — spiritual lectures open to all
  • Free prasadam — sanctified vegan and vegetarian food prepared with love
  • Cultural programs — dance, music and spiritual demonstrations

Who Organises It?

Ratha Yatra Berlin 2026 is organised by Hare Krishna Berlin Devotees — a devotional community in Berlin following the teachings of His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Srila Prabhupada.

Our seva partners include Indo German Spectrum, Gujarati Samaj Berlin, Hindu Gemeinde Berlin, UP Pravasi Samiti Berlin, Berlin Bihar Gharana and other community organisations.

How to Participate

Simply come on the day — no registration required for attending. However if you would like to volunteer or help us prepare prasadam, please register on our volunteer page.
